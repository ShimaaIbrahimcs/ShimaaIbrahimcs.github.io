I am a Research Associate and Software Engineer specializing in ontologies, with a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Bonn and over six years of experience in ontology engineering, semantic web technologies, and research data management. My work focuses on developing open-source frameworks for ontology matching, enrichment, and quality assessment across multilingual and metadata-rich domains. I have strong technical expertise in semantic modeling using RDF, OWL, and SPARQL, as well as practical experience in implementing ontology-based solutions with Scala, Java, and Python. My research contributes to FAIR data practices and supports semantic interoperability in domains such as scholarly communication, biomedical data, and fiscal information—with solutions that can applied effectively to any domain.

Projects

  • MoMatch – A multilingual ontology matching framework that identifies and generates high-quality alignments between ontologies in different natural languages, supporting semantic interoperability in cross-lingual settings.
  • QASO – A comprehensive metric-based suite for assessing the quality of multilingual ontology enrichment, covering ontology structure, matching accuracy, and enrichment effectiveness.
  • LAMBDA – EU Horizon 2020 (GA no. 809965): An international project focused on knowledge exchange and collaboration between institutions in Serbia, Germany, and the UK. Contributed to strengthening R&D capabilities at the Mihajlo Pupin Institute through semantic technologies, training, and dissemination activities.
  • MULON – A multilingual ontology merging tool that integrates heterogeneous ontologies in different languages into a unified multilingual ontology, facilitating cross-lingual reasoning and knowledge sharing.
  • OECM – An ontology enrichment tool that automates the integration of concepts and relations from source ontologies in different languages to enhance the coverage and expressiveness of a target ontology.
  • CLOE – A cross-lingual ontology enrichment framework based on a multi-agent architecture. It enables the enrichment of ontologies using multilingual textual resources, improving multilingual knowledge access and semantic interoperability.
